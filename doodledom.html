About

Say hello to Doodledom, one of the lesser known gems of the Fherheldihite Empire. Founded in 1300 in the year of our lord Cthulhu, Doodledom sits on the edge of the historic battle-site, Lovelace's Lament. It's picturesque hills and historic charm makes Doodledom a place for the whole family. Today Doodledom is welcoming the new era by trying to bring modern-day tourists back to the time of the Great Revolts of 1887 alc through its use of 24/7 live renactments, where the whole town dresses and acts like they came straight from the 19th century (we may still be rebelling). We here at Doodledom hope that you find our town not only an enjoyable place to stay, but also a great place learn about our Empire's history.

Places to Visit

As Doodledom enters its 702nd birthday, many of our famous landmarks have been listed below as a way for you to navigate our town with more ease. Each landmark represents a time in our long history in which you can discover how our Great Empire of Fherheldihite came to be.

Doodledom National Bank

Estd 1357 in the year of our lord Cthulu

Created in 1357, this bank stands as a testament to our great nation and its ability to stand the test of time. With vaulted ceilings and 24-K gold interior, our bank was created to show the prosperity of the town. Similarly, in our great nation, we use snips, if you plan on visiting, I would suggest that you come visit the bank, not only for the historical value, but also for all your banking needs!

Altar to the Lord Cthulu

Estd unknown...

Said to have been delivered by Cthulu himself, the altar is comprised of over 1,200 human remains said to have been sacrifices to him during the Plague of the Innocent in which many of our faithful citizens became cannibalistic due to the shortage of meat. They gave the left over meat to Cthulhu as a way to keep the plague away.

Battle of Lovelace's Lament

Estd 1900

Many brave men have lost their lives to this field. Fought in 1900 Lovelace's Lament symbolized the turning point in the Great Revolution as the great Fherheldihite army changed their attack strategy from one of quieting rebels to fighting a full fledged army of citizens. Today the battlefield serves as a reminder of our great nation and it's army.

City Hall

Estd 1300

Designed and created by Sir William J Smith founder of Doodledom and creator of our national anthem, "Doodle Doodledom" written in the true language of our forefathers, Doodledonian. Here at City Hall our representatives still must used the old language as a way of "preserving the spirits of our ancestors"; although many do not understand what they are saying, they represent the true values our town holds dear.

If I do not respect the Lord Cthulhu, will I be smitted?

We advise all possible visitors to be fully aware that the Lord Cthulhu is accepting, but only to those that fear and worship him. In turn, if you are not a supporter of the Lord, he will eat you in your sleep. Our police chief has advised us to give this warning before anymore tourists are eaten.




How many people in Doodledom speak the old language?

We are unsure of the exact number, but many of our citizens are unable to communicate with tourists due to the differences in the two languages. If you wish to visit please do bring a translation dictionary. If you are unable to buy one before your trip, be advised that we sell books for 175 snips on our City Hall's website.
